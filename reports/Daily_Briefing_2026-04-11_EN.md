This article introduces "Skelebones," a novel rigging system designed to capture and control non-rigid deformations in 4D shapes, addressing the limitations of existing free-form bones and kinematic structures. The core innovation lies in a three-step process that balances expressive deformation with intuitive control.

The technical implementation begins with "Bones," where temporally consistent deformable Gaussians are compressed to form free-form bones that accurately represent non-rigid surface movements.
