**Background**

The article addresses a known limitation in standard multi-head attention (MHA) mechanisms, commonly used in visual perception models like Vision Transformers (ViTs). The core issue identified is that the conventional softmax activation function, while standard, can lead to "noisy" attention patterns. This noise can dilute the importance of truly relevant features and consequently hinder the overall performance of the model. The proposed solution, Denoising Attention (DnA), aims to mitigate this by introducing a more sophisticated attention mechanism.

**Technical Implementation**

DnA introduces a novel approach to attention calculation by employing two distinct query types: a "positive query" and a "negative query." The positive query is designed to specifically pinpoint image features that align with the correct class. Conversely, the negative query is engineered to identify features that are closely related but ultimately irrelevant to the target class. The key innovation lies in how these interactions are processed: DnA projects the attention scores derived from these positive and negative queries into two separate subspaces. Crucially, these subspaces are designed to have larger principal angles between them. This geometric separation is intended to promote enhanced discriminability by clearly distinguishing relevant from irrelevant features, thereby "denoising" the attention process.

**Application Scenarios**

The effectiveness of DnA is demonstrated across several visual understanding tasks. When integrated with a ViT-B backbone, DnA achieved a notable absolute performance gain of 0.8% on the ImageNet-1K benchmark. Further experiments highlight its versatility, showing improvements in video understanding tasks. Specifically, DnA yielded a 1.8% gain when applied to video transformers and a 0.5% gain in the context of video Large Language Models (LLMs). These results suggest that DnA's denoising capabilities are beneficial for both static image analysis and more complex temporal visual reasoning.

**Summary**

The Denoising Attention (DnA) mechanism offers a promising enhancement to standard multi-head attention in visual models. By leveraging distinct positive and negative queries to project interactions into geometrically separated subspaces, DnA effectively reduces attention noise and improves feature discriminability. This approach has shown tangible performance improvements on ImageNet-1K and extends to video understanding tasks, indicating its potential as a valuable component for advancing visual perception systems.
