Furthermore, direct similarity-based clustering can lead to fragmented representations, negatively impacting the quality of pooled visual information.

First, it employs a diversity-driven strategy to refine attention-based token selection, aiming for a more representative subset of tokens. Second, it introduces Spatio-temporal Rotary Position Embedding (ST-RoPE). This embedding technique is designed to preserve the inherent geometric structure of video data by leveraging locality priors, thus mitigating the distortion issues encountered in traditional clustering methods.

A key finding is its efficiency: when retaining a mere 10% of the original video tokens, Tango achieved remarkable results, preserving 98.9% of the performance on the LLaVA-OV benchmark. This efficiency gain is accompanied by a substantial inference speedup of 1.88x, highlighting its practical utility in real-world applications.

**Summary**

In summary, this work presents Tango, a novel token pruning framework that significantly enhances the efficiency of Video LLMs. By addressing the shortcomings of existing attention-based and similarity-based methods with a diversity-driven selection strategy and ST-RoPE, Tango effectively optimizes visual signal utilization. **Background**

The article identifies a critical shift in document parsing requirements driven by the rise of AI agents. Traditional parsing methods, often focused on syntactic accuracy or simple text extraction, are becoming insufficient. The core technical challenge now lies in achieving **semantic correctness**, meaning the parsed output must accurately represent the structure and meaning of the original document to enable autonomous decision-making by AI agents. This includes preserving intricate details like table structures, precise chart data, semantically relevant formatting, and visual context. Existing benchmarks are deemed inadequate as they often use limited document types and rely on text-similarity metrics that fail to detect agent-critical errors.

**Technical Implementation**

To address this gap, the authors introduce **ParseBench**, a novel benchmark comprising approximately 2,000 human-verified pages from diverse enterprise documents (insurance, finance, government). ParseBench is structured around five key capability dimensions: tables, charts, content faithfulness, semantic formatting, and visual grounding. This comprehensive evaluation framework allows for a granular assessment of parsing system performance. The benchmark was used to evaluate 14 different methods, including vision-language models, specialized document parsers, and LlamaParse. The results indicate a fragmented landscape of current capabilities, with no single method demonstrating consistent strength across all five dimensions.

**Application Scenarios**

The findings from ParseBench have direct implications for enterprise automation. The benchmark's focus on semantic correctness and its diverse document coverage make it highly relevant for real-world applications where AI agents need to interpret complex documents. Scenarios such as automated claims processing in insurance, financial report analysis, and government document management would benefit from parsers that excel in preserving table structures, accurately extracting chart data, and understanding the nuances of document formatting and visual elements. The identified capability gaps highlight areas for future research and development in building more robust and reliable AI agents for these domains.

**Summary**

The article effectively argues for the necessity of semantically correct document parsing for AI agents and introduces ParseBench as a solution to evaluate this crucial aspect. The benchmark's comprehensive nature and diverse dataset reveal that current parsing technologies are not uniformly capable across essential dimensions like tables, charts, content faithfulness, semantic formatting, and visual grounding. While LlamaParse Agentic shows promising overall performance, significant capability gaps remain, underscoring the ongoing challenge of building AI agents that can reliably understand and act upon complex enterprise documents.

**Background**

Current large foundation models show promise in embodied intelligence for household tasks, leveraging egocentric input for synthesis and reasoning. However, a significant challenge lies in the inherent noisiness of Visual-Language Model (VLM)-based auto-labeling. This noise originates from a lack of accurate human action labels, chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning, and precise spatial annotations in primary data sources. These deficiencies are exacerbated when dealing with long-horizon spatial instruction following, leading to VLM reasoning chains and world-model synthesis that can hallucinate objects, omit critical steps, or disregard real-world physical constraints. The root cause is insufficient data coverage for minute-long daily household planning tasks and inaccurate spatial grounding.

**Technical Implementation**

To overcome these limitations, the EgoTL framework has been developed. EgoTL introduces a think-aloud capture pipeline for egocentric data. Its core components include a "say-before-act" protocol that records step-by-step goals and spoken reasoning with word-level timestamps. This is complemented by physical property calibration using metric-scale spatial estimators, a memory-bank walkthrough for scene context, and clip-level tags for detailed navigation instructions and manipulation actions. This comprehensive data collection approach aims to provide richer, more accurate, and spatially grounded information.

**Application Scenarios**

EgoTL enables robust benchmarking of VLMs and World Models across six task dimensions, spanning three layers of abstraction and long-horizon generation over minute-long sequences. This allows for evaluation on over 100 daily household tasks. Initial findings indicate that current foundation models still struggle to perform effectively as egocentric assistants or open-world simulators. However, fine-tuning these models with human CoT aligned with metric labels from the EgoTL training split demonstrates significant improvements in long-horizon planning and reasoning, step-wise reasoning, instruction following, and spatial grounding.

**Summary**

EgoTL addresses critical data quality issues in embodied intelligence for household tasks by introducing a sophisticated egocentric data capture pipeline. By integrating spoken reasoning, precise spatial calibration, and detailed action tagging, EgoTL provides a more accurate and grounded dataset. This enables more effective benchmarking of foundation models and demonstrates that fine-tuning with this enriched data can substantially enhance their capabilities in complex, long-horizon tasks, paving the way for more competent egocentric assistants and simulators.

**Background**

The article addresses a critical challenge in applying prompt learning to vision-language models (VLMs) in real-world scenarios: robustness to label noise. While prompt learning offers parameter efficiency, its susceptibility to noisy labels, particularly in the text prompts, hinders its reliability. The core insight is that visual content, being inherently richer and more stable, can serve as a grounding mechanism to mitigate the impact of noisy textual supervision.

**Technical Implementation**

VisPrompt introduces a novel vision-guided prompt learning framework. Its key innovation lies in a cross-modal attention mechanism that injects visual semantics directly into prompt representations. This allows prompt tokens to dynamically attend to and aggregate relevant visual information from the input image, effectively anchoring prompt learning to stable instance-level visual evidence. To further enhance robustness, a conditional modulation mechanism is employed. This mechanism adaptively controls the strength of visual information injection based on the quality of visual cues, striking a better balance between pre-trained text knowledge and image-specific evidence. This adaptive approach prevents over-reliance on potentially noisy visual data and stabilizes prompt updates.

**Application Scenarios**

The proposed VisPrompt framework is particularly relevant for applications where datasets are prone to label noise, such as large-scale image classification, object detection, and image captioning tasks. By enhancing robustness, VisPrompt enables the effective use of VLMs in scenarios with imperfect annotations, reducing the need for extensive manual data cleaning. The framework's ability to significantly improve robustness while keeping the VLM backbone frozen and adding minimal trainable parameters makes it a practical and efficient solution for deployment.

**Summary**

VisPrompt presents a compelling solution for improving the robustness of prompt learning in VLMs under label noise. By leveraging cross-modal attention to inject visual semantics and employing adaptive conditional modulation, the framework effectively grounds prompt learning in stable visual evidence. This approach demonstrably suppresses noise-induced disturbances, reduces instability, and alleviates memorization of mislabeled samples, leading to significant performance gains and enhanced robustness across multiple benchmarks. The parameter-efficient nature of VisPrompt makes it a highly practical and scalable technique for real-world vision-language tasks.

### 5. [VisionFoundry: Teaching VLMs Visual Perception with Synthetic Images](https://arxiv.org/abs/2604.09531v1)
👤 **Authors:** Guanyu Zhou, Yida Yin, Wenhao Chai
<details>
<summary><strong>📄 Paper Summary:</strong> **Background**

Vision-language models (VLMs) face challenges in visual perception tasks, ...</summary>

**Background**

Vision-language models (VLMs) face challenges in visual perception tasks, particularly in spatial understanding and viewpoint recognition. This limitation is attributed to insufficient low-level visual skill supervision in natural image datasets. The core technical question explored is whether targeted synthetic supervision, derived solely from task keywords like "Depth Order," can effectively mitigate these weaknesses.

**Technical Implementation**

The proposed solution, VisionFoundry, is a task-aware synthetic data generation pipeline. It operates by taking a task name as input and leveraging large language models (LLMs) to generate associated questions, answers, and text-to-image (T2I) prompts. These prompts are then used by T2I models to synthesize images. A proprietary VLM is employed to verify the consistency of the generated image-question-answer triples, eliminating the need for reference images or human annotation. This process results in the creation of VisionFoundry-10K, a synthetic VQA dataset comprising 10,000 image-question-answer triples across 10 distinct tasks.

**Application Scenarios**

Training VLMs on the VisionFoundry-10K dataset demonstrates significant performance gains on visual perception benchmarks, with improvements of +7% on MMVP and +10% on CV-Bench-3D. Notably, these gains are achieved while preserving broader VLM capabilities and exhibiting favorable scaling behavior with increasing data volume. This suggests that task-targeted synthetic supervision is a potent strategy for addressing VLM bottlenecks and facilitating more systematic training.

**Summary**

VisionFoundry presents a novel approach to VLM training by generating synthetic data tailored to specific visual perception tasks. By utilizing LLMs and T2I models, it bypasses the need for extensive human annotation and reference images. The resulting VisionFoundry-10K dataset has proven effective in enhancing VLM performance on spatial understanding and viewpoint recognition tasks, highlighting the potential of synthetic supervision for advancing VLM capabilities.
