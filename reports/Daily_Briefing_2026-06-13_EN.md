Current image generation models excel at single-image creation and editing, achieving high photorealism and instruction adherence. However, they are architecturally limited in generating interleaved text-image sequences, a capability vital for visual storytelling, step-by-step guidance, and embodied AI tasks. Existing Unified Multimodal Models (UMMs) also show deficiencies in this area. This paper introduces InterleaveThinker, a novel multi-agent pipeline designed to equip any existing image generator with interleaved generation functionality.

**Technical Implementation**

InterleaveThinker utilizes a two-agent architecture. A **planner agent** orchestrates the text-image input sequence, directing the image generator at each stage. Reinforcement learning (RL) is then applied via Interleave-Critic-RL-13k, using GRPO to enhance step-wise instruction correction within a generation trajectory. To address the computational challenge of optimizing long generation trajectories (potentially exceeding 25 generator calls), the system employs accuracy and step-wise rewards, enabling single-step RL to effectively guide the entire process.

InterleaveThinker's primary application is enabling complex visual narratives and sequential image generation. This includes creating step-by-step tutorials, generating visual explanations for multi-stage processes, or supporting embodied agents in tasks requiring sequential visual feedback. The system's ability to refine instructions based on generated output makes it robust for scenarios where initial generation might not perfectly align with the intended sequence. Furthermore, the observed performance gains on reasoning-based benchmarks suggest potential for applications in visual problem-solving and complex instruction following.

**Summary**

InterleaveThinker represents a significant advancement in image generation by introducing a flexible multi-agent pipeline that imbues existing generators with interleaved generation capabilities. Through a combination of planning, critical evaluation, and RL-driven refinement, it effectively addresses the limitations of current models in generating sequential visual content.
