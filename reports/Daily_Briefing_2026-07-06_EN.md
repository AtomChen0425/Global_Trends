These challenges stem from the substantial cost and effort required to curate diverse training datasets and the inherent scalability limitations of current methodologies. Addressing this gap, the Align4D framework is introduced to facilitate the translation of any-modal input into synchronized video-3D pairs, thereby enabling coherent 4D generation.

**Technical Implementation**
Align4D employs a novel approach by leveraging video to direct 4D motion and 3D data to define 4D geometry. Three core techniques underpin its functionality: Object Distance Alignment, which involves searching for Video-Aligned and Multiview-Aligned Object Distances (VAOD/MAOD) to ensure consistency between 4D renderings, video inputs, and the priors of multiview diffusion models. Motion-Geometry Joint Alignment is crucial for synchronizing known and unknown views using both video and 3D inputs, guaranteeing coherent 4D output. Finally, Asynchronous Optimization decouples the training of Gaussian attributes and deformation networks, leading to improved fidelity in both motion and geometry. The framework is further supported by the X4D dataset, designed for benchmarking X-to-4D generation with integrated prompt, image, video, and 3D data.

**Application Scenarios**
The primary application of Align4D lies in enabling flexible and high-quality X-to-4D generation across various modalities. This framework is particularly valuable for scenarios requiring the creation of dynamic 3D assets from diverse inputs, such as generating animated 3D models from textual descriptions, animating static 3D objects based on video, or reconstructing 4D scenes from a combination of images and videos. The ability to achieve state-of-the-art quality and consistency in these complex generation tasks makes Align4D a promising tool for fields like virtual reality, augmented reality, content creation, and digital twins.
