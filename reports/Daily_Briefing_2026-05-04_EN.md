### 1. [Posterior Augmented Flow Matching](https://arxiv.org/abs/2605.00825v1)
👤 **Authors:** George Stoica, Sayak Paul, Matthew Wallingford

<summary><strong>📄 Paper Summary:</strong> **Background**

**Background**

Flow Matching (FM) is a generative modeling technique that learns a time-dependent vector field to transform samples from a simple prior distribution to a complex data distribution. While effective, FM faces significant challenges in high-dimensional domains like image generation. The core issue lies in the sparse and high-variance training signal. Each training sample only provides supervision for a single trajectory and an intermediate point, leading to an under-constrained learning process. This can result in "flow collapse," where the model memorizes specific source-target mappings, producing overly similar outputs and hindering generalization.

**Technical Implementation**

To address these limitations, Posterior-Augmented Flow Matching (PAFM) is proposed. PAFM generalizes FM by replacing single-target supervision with an expectation over an approximate posterior of valid target completions for any given intermediate state and condition. This intractable posterior is factorized into two components: the likelihood of the intermediate state given a hypothesized endpoint, and the prior probability of that endpoint under the condition. PAFM then employs an importance sampling strategy to construct a mixture of multiple plausible target completions. This approach is theoretically proven to provide an unbiased estimator of the original FM objective while significantly reducing gradient variance during training. By aggregating information from numerous potential continuation trajectories for each intermediate point, PAFM enhances the robustness of the learning process.

**Application Scenarios and Summary**

PAFM demonstrates superior performance compared to standard FM across various configurations. Experimental results show improvements of up to 3.4 FID50K on benchmarks like ImageNet and CC12M, across different model scales (SiT-B/2 and SiT-XL/2) and architectures (SiT and MMDiT), for both class-conditional and text-conditional generation.
