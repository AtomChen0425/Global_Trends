The proposed OmniAgent framework is designed for complex long video understanding tasks where computational efficiency and nuanced reasoning are critical. The agent's performance improvements with more reasoning turns suggest its applicability in scenarios requiring deep analysis and iterative refinement of understanding.

**Background**

The deployment of multimodal foundation models (MFMs) as closed-loop policies faces a critical challenge: conditioning actions on observations that are not immediately visible. Current benchmarks often fall short by either revealing the complete state, blending hidden-state reconstruction with other agent capabilities, or assessing recall only post-episode. This limits our ability to accurately measure and improve an MFM's capacity for memory and sequential reasoning.

**Technical Implementation**

To address this, RNG-Bench (Reconstructive Non-Markov Games) has been developed. This benchmark suite isolates and evaluates an MFM's ability to reconstruct past observations and utilize them for multi-step interactions. It features two games: "Matching Pairs" tests recall of briefly revealed visual information, and "3D Maze" requires integrating egocentric views into a spatial map. **Application Scenarios and Summary**

RNG-Bench provides a robust framework for assessing and advancing MFMs in scenarios requiring memory and sequential decision-making.
